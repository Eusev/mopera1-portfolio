Abstract

In this study, the development of a web-based multi-purpose cooperative information system was undertaken to enhance the operational efficiency and member engagement of multi-purpose cooperatives. The system included modules for member registration, loan processing, and information dissemination, all accessible through a user-friendly web interface. A key feature of the system was the integration of geo-mapping, which enabled cooperatives to visualize member distributions.

A repeated design and development process were employed, incorporating stress testing, compatibility testing, and User Acceptance Testing (UAT). The UAT results demonstrated high levels of user satisfaction with the system’s usability, functional suitability and reliability. Both members and administrators found the system effective in streamlining processes and enhancing communication.

The study concluded that the developed system significantly improved the cooperative’s ability to manage its operations, leading to better service provision for its members. Future recommendations include the integration of mobile application support and advanced data analytics to further enhance the system’s functionalities.

Scope and Limitation

The study focused on developing the Multi-Purpose Cooperative Information system as a web-based application with several capabilities.

Member Management. The system allowed cooperative members to manage their membership information, including personal details, and membership status.

Loan Management. This feature streamlined the loan process, from application to approval. Members could apply for loans online simply by filling out an online loan application form indicating the loan type, amount, term in months, and adding their purpose of loans, and were able to track their application status. Coop admins monitored these loan applications, checked the forms submitted by members and had the capability to approve or reject the members’ loan application while adding the reason for rejection. If a member’s loan application has been processed by the admin, they are able to see their applications’ status by going to the loan history section in their account page.

Communication and Messaging. This enabled communication between the cooperative and its members through announcements and messaging features, facilitating the dissemination of important announcements, updates, and notifications.

Geo-location. The system integrated Google Maps API, which allowed the cooperative to view heat maps showing the locations of its office branches, distribution of members, active and inactive member counts.

Security and Access Control. Role-based access control was implemented to restrict system access based on user roles and responsibilities. Additionally, the web-based application was mobile responsive, ensuring accessibility across different devices.

However, the study and system development were limited to Jeannie’s Touch Multi-purpose Cooperative. While the GPS feature provided valuable geographical data, it should be noted that it did not provide exact locations. The GPS data represented general areas rather than specific addresses or locations.

The system managed loan processes but did not integrate with the payroll system, although it did provide transactional receipts for conducted transactions. Furthermore, while supporting membership applications and loan management, the system did not include functionalities for Human Resources Management, Financial Management, or processes for terminating memberships.
